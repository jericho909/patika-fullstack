Brahmin Steak

Transport yourself to the rugged landscapes of the post-apocalyptic wasteland with this hearty recipe for Brahmin Steak, a culinary homage to the survivalist spirit of the Fallout series. As you sizzle succulent cuts of Brahmin (or beef, for those outside the wasteland) on the grill, envision the crackling flames and smoky aroma that permeate the air of your makeshift campsite. Let your taste buds rejoice as you indulge in tender slices of meat, paired with a savory medley of sautéed vegetables infused with Worcestershire sauce and beef broth. Each bite is a testament to resilience and resourcefulness, echoing the ingenuity of wasteland dwellers who thrive amidst adversity. Ingredients:

  • 2 large Brahmin steaks (or beef steaks, if Brahmin is unavailable)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the Brahmin steaks with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the steaks for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Remove from heat and let rest for a few minutes.
  4. While the steaks are resting, heat the remaining olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
  5. Add the sliced onion, bell pepper, and mushrooms to the skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and beef broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Simmer for 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Remove the skillet from heat and stir in the butter until melted and incorporated.
  8. Slice the grilled Brahmin steaks against the grain and serve with the sautéed vegetables on top.
  9. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
